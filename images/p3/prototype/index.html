What's Emphemeral Messaging?

Ephemeral (ə-FEM-ər-əl) was originally a medical term with the specific meaning "lasting only one day," as a fever or sickness (Hemera means "day" in Greek.) The word became more general, coming to mean "lasting a short time," covering the life spans of plants or insects and then eventually anything that is fleeting or transitory. A related word is the plural noun ephemera, meaning "things that are meant to last for only a short time." Posters for a rock concert are often ephemera, unless the band is so famous that they get saved and sold on eBay. Ephemeral – describes something that only lasts for a short period of time. Messaging – sending and receiving images, text, etc. in conversation Ephemeral messaging – transmission of multimedia messages that ‘self-destruct’ after a period of time, vs regular texting and messaging that stay there or need to be manually deleted The thing about ephemeral messaging is that it’s the more recent development in digital communication, and may change how we interact through technology.
